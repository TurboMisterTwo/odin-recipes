A plate of Polish Pierogi

A tradtional Polish dish and a family favourite. These can be quite a lot of work
so are normally only made for special occasions/holidays. Simple ingredients
and a bit of elbow grease result in a fantastic little dish. These are best served
fried, but do have to be boiled first.

Ingredients

    Potato Filling
  • 84g butter
  • 150g chopped onions
  • 840g cold mashed potatoes
  • 2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoon white pepper
    Dough
  • 225g sour cream
  • 3 large eggs
  • 360g flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Steps

  1. Make potato filling: Melt butter in the same skillet over medium heat. Cook
    and stir onion in hot butter until translucent, about 5 minutes. Stir in mashed
    potatoes, salt, and white pepper. Remove from heat.

  2. Make dough: Beat together sour cream and eggs in a large bowl until smooth.
    Sift flour, baking powder, and salt into a separate bowl; stir into sour cream
    mixture until dough comes together.

  3. Knead dough on a lightly floured surface until firm and smooth.

  4. Roll out dough to 3mm thickness. Cut into 7.5cm rounds using a biscuit
    cutter. Place a small spoonful of potato filling into the center of each round.

  5. Moisten edges with water, fold over, and press with a fork to seal.

  6. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Add pierogies in batches
    to boiling water and cook until they float to the top, 3 to 5 minutes. Remove
    with a slotted spoon.

  7. Enjoy!
